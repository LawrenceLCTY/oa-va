Voice Assistant Architecture Tradeoffs

Stakeholder summary: no single architecture maximizes latency, privacy, quality, safety, openness, and cost at the same time.

Architecture Live Conversation Feel Voice Quality Clinical Safety Control Privacy / Data Control Cost Best Use Main Compromise
Browser Voice + Deterministic Engine Browser STT/TTS, local protocol engine Medium Basic High Medium Very low Fallback mode, cheapest demo, low setup Voice and transcription quality vary by browser and device.
Local Open-Source Pipeline Local STT -> deterministic engine -> local TTS Medium Good High High Low to medium Private data, open-source preference, auditability Needs GPU/model operations; less smooth than realtime cloud voice.
Cloud Cascaded Pipeline Cloud STT/LLM/TTS, local clinical engine Medium High High Medium Medium Good production quality without heavy local model ops Recurring vendor cost and privacy/compliance review.
Realtime Cloud Voice + Clinical Tools Live speech-to-speech, local tool/state callbacks Best High Medium-high Medium High Most natural call experience and stakeholder demos Best UX, but highest vendor dependency and usage cost.
Local End-to-End Audio Model Covo-style private voice-to-voice service Promising Potentially high Medium High GPU-heavy Research path toward private natural voice Experimental, harder to audit, and operationally heavy.
Fine-Tuned Monolithic Chatbot One model controls conversation and advice High High Risky Depends High Not recommended as clinical authority Hard to prove no hallucination, no missed fields, and safe escalation.
Recommended
Hybrid Safety-First Architecture Deterministic clinical core + swappable voice/model backends 		Configurable Configurable High Configurable Configurable Realistic production path across demos, pilots, and private deployment Requires product choice per deployment: fastest, cheapest, and most private cannot all be maximized together.
Bottom line: keep the clinical workflow, red-flag escalation, and doctor report deterministic. Use cloud realtime voice when the priority is the smoothest user experience; use local open-source models when the priority is private data and deployment control.
