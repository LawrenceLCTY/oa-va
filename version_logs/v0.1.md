# v0.1 Version Log

Date: 2026-06-18

## Goal

Build the first local prototype of an osteoarthritis home pain check-in voice assistant.

The prototype should confirm basic identity, assess pain with a 0-10 scale plus functional anchoring, ask about side effects and urgent red flags, advise urgent care when needed, and generate a structured doctor report.

## User-Facing Changes

- Added local browser app at `http://127.0.0.1:8000`.
- Added typed chat input.
- Added browser speech recognition when supported.
- Added browser text-to-speech when supported.
- Added preferred female browser voice selection when available.
- Added hands-free voice flow after check-in start:
  - assistant speaks
  - browser listens
  - recognized answer is submitted automatically
- Updated assistant introduction to:

```text
Hello, I'm a researcher from Peking University Medical Hospital.
I'm calling for a short joint pain check-in.
May I confirm your name, mobile number, and age?
```

- Shortened assistant prompts to sound more like a real phone call and less like an AI chatbot.
- Improved page/chat scrolling behavior.
- Added report display and local report save action.

## Conversation Flow Implemented

The assistant currently asks for:

1. Name, mobile number, and age.
2. Current joint pain score from 0 to 10.
3. Pain location.
4. Functional impact.
5. Whether pain is better, worse, or about the same as usual.
6. Current treatment context.
7. Side effects or new symptoms.
8. Urgent red-flag symptoms.

After the final answer, the app generates a doctor-readable report.

## Safety and Clinical Rules

Implemented deterministic urgent red-flag handling for:

- chest pain
- trouble breathing
- stroke-like symptoms
- severe allergic reaction
- vomiting blood
- black or bloody stools
- fainting or severe dizziness
- confusion
- fever with a hot swollen joint
- severe symptoms after injection or treatment
- inability to stand/walk/bear weight when linked to fall, injury, trauma, or accident

If a severe red flag is detected, the app advises urgent medical care and records the red flag in the report.

The app does not diagnose, prescribe, or adjust medication.

## Validation Layer

Added internal validation with four possible decisions:

- `ACCEPT`
- `ASK_AGAIN`
- `CLARIFY`
- `ESCALATE`

Implemented examples:

- Rejects invalid pain score answers such as `coffee`.
- Treats severe but non-numeric pain phrases such as `hurts like hell` as ambiguous and asks for a 0-10 number.
- Detects pain score `0` plus severe functional limitation as a contradiction and asks for confirmation.
- Detects pain score `0` plus `worse than usual` as a contradiction and asks for confirmation.
- Rejects unclear side-effect and safety-screen answers.
- Escalates deterministic red-flag symptoms without relying on an LLM.

## Report Output

The report includes:

- Patient Identity
- Call Metadata
- Pain Assessment
- Side Effects / Symptoms Reported
- Urgent Red Flags
- Summary for Doctor
- Suggested Follow-up Priority
- Limitations

Reports can be saved locally to the `reports/` directory.

## Optional Local LLM Status

The app does not require an LLM to run.

The optional local model path recorded for future integration is:

```text
/home/lawrencelcty/huggingface/models/Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507-FP8
```

`app/llm.py` supports a local OpenAI-compatible endpoint through:

```bash
LOCAL_LLM_URL
LOCAL_LLM_MODEL
LOCAL_LLM_TIMEOUT_SECONDS
```

The LLM may polish wording or report text, but deterministic validation and clinical safety rules remain the source of truth.

## Known Limitations

- Browser speech support varies by browser and operating system.
- TTS voice selection depends on installed/exposed browser voices.
- Hands-free voice flow may still be affected by browser microphone permissions and autoplay restrictions.
- The app uses in-memory session state only.
- Saved reports are local files only.
- The validation layer is still rule-based and may miss unusual language.
- The local Qwen model is not enabled by default.
- No authentication, consent workflow, encryption, audit logging, or production privacy controls are implemented.
- No clinician dashboard or real EHR/clinical integration is implemented.
- The app is a prototype and not a medical device.
